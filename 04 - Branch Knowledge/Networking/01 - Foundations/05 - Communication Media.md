# Communication Media

---

# 1. Purpose

To understand the physical paths that carry signals between network devices and how different communication media affect network communication.

---

# 2. Central Question

How do physical signals travel from one device to another?

---

# 3. Problem Statement

After digital data has been encoded into physical signals, those signals require a medium through which they can travel.

Different environments require different communication media depending on distance, speed, cost, reliability, and interference.

---

# 4. Definition

A communication medium is the physical or wireless path through which encoded signals travel between network devices.

---

# 5. Core Idea

Communication media do not understand data or protocols.

They simply transport physical signals from one location to another.

# 7. Components

## Copper Cable (Ethernet)

Carries electrical signals.

---

## Fiber Optic Cable

Carries light pulses.

---

## Wireless Media

Carries radio waves.

Examples:

- Wi-Fi
- Bluetooth
- Cellular

---

# 8. Mechanism

1. Data becomes bits.
2. Bits are encoded into signals.
3. Signals enter the communication medium.
4. The medium carries the signals.
5. The receiving device detects the signals.
6. Signals are decoded back into bits.

# 10. Mental Model

Think of roads.

Cars carry people.

Roads carry cars.

Roads do not know where people are going.

Similarly, communication media carry signals but do not interpret them.

Investigators consider the communication medium when analyzing signal loss, interception, or physical connectivity issues.

# 20. Common Misconceptions

- Communication media do not transport bits directly.
- Wi-Fi is a communication medium, not a protocol.
- Fiber transmits light, not electricity.
